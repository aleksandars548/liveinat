AMS and WAFF Support in Austria

10 min readLast updated 2026-05-06

AMS and WAFF can help with work and training questions, but eligibility depends on your situation and current programme rules.

Disclaimer: This guide is informational only and not legal, tax, immigration, medical, financial or professional advice. Rules can change. Always check the official Austrian source before applying.

AMS and WAFF are two important names for job seekers and people considering training in Austria, especially in Vienna. They are useful, but they do not guarantee funding or eligibility.

Rules can change. Always check the official AMS or WAFF source before applying or enrolling in a course.

1. What AMS is

AMS is Austria's public employment service. It may support job search, unemployment-related topics, training information and labour market orientation depending on your status.

2. What WAFF is

WAFF is connected to Vienna and focuses on work-related development, qualifications and training support for eligible people.

3. Who they help

Eligibility can depend on residence, employment status, work history, location and programme rules. Ask directly instead of assuming you qualify.

4. Training and courses

AMS and WAFF may list courses or support training in certain cases. Do not pay for a course expecting reimbursement unless you have official confirmation.

5. Vienna-specific WAFF support

WAFF provides information for new immigrants and a course search. Check current Vienna-specific options before applying.

6. How to prepare before contacting them

Prepare your CV, ID, certificates, employment history, German level and clear questions. Bring official letters if you have them.

Practical checklist

  • Check official eligibility.
  • Prepare CV and documents.
  • Define your job or training goal.
  • Ask about approval before starting a course.
  • Keep written decisions.
  • Continue your own job search.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Assuming support is automatic.
  • Starting courses before approval.
  • Arriving without documents.
  • Missing appointment instructions.
  • Expecting services to replace active applications.
