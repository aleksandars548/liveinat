How to Find an Apartment in Vienna

12 min readLast updated 2026-05-06

Find housing in Vienna with a clearer search strategy, better viewing questions and fewer registration surprises.

Disclaimer: This guide is informational only and not legal, tax, immigration, medical, financial or professional advice. Rules can change. Always check the official Austrian source before applying.

Vienna is livable and well connected, but apartment hunting can be competitive. Newcomers need to understand listings, documents, deposits, contracts and whether the address can support Anmeldung or Meldezettel paperwork.

This guide is practical orientation, not legal advice. Rental rules can change. Always check official information or qualified tenant advice before making high-value decisions.

1. Where to search

Use a mix of property portals, housing groups, student housing, employer contacts, relocation services and direct provider websites. Track listings, replies and questions in one document.

2. Documents landlords may ask for

Landlords may request ID, proof of income, employment or study confirmation and references. If you are new to Austria, explain your situation clearly and provide reasonable alternatives.

3. Deposit and upfront costs

Deposits and upfront costs vary. Do not transfer money until you have verified the apartment, the person offering it and the agreement. Keep payment records.

4. Rental contract basics

Read the contract carefully. Check rent, operating costs, utilities, duration, notice rules, furniture, repairs and handover conditions. If you do not understand German contract wording, get help before signing.

5. Viewing checklist

Check heating, windows, water pressure, noise, internet, laundry, building condition, transport and local shops. Ask what is included and whether registration at the address is possible.

6. Red flags

Be careful with pressure, copied photos, requests for money before viewing, landlords who cannot show the property and offers that seem unrealistically cheap.

7. Anmeldung and Meldezettel importance

If you need address registration, confirm before signing that the accommodation provider can support the necessary paperwork.

Practical checklist

  • Define budget and commute limits.
  • Prepare a renter profile.
  • Ask about registration support.
  • Inspect the apartment carefully.
  • Review contract terms.
  • Verify before paying.
  • Keep signed documents and receipts.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Ignoring total monthly cost.
  • Forgetting the Meldezettel question.
  • Paying before verification.
  • Skipping contract review.
  • Sending generic application messages.

FAQ

Can foreigners rent in Vienna?

Yes, many do. Landlords may ask for documents proving identity, income or study status.

Should I pay before viewing?

Be very cautious. Verify the property and agreement before transferring money.

Why does Meldezettel matter for housing?

Some official and practical steps may depend on address registration. Ask before signing.
