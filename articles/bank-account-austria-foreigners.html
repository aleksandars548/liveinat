Opening a Bank Account in Austria as a Foreigner

10 min readLast updated 2026-05-06

A bank account can help with salary, rent and bills, but the right option depends on your documents and needs.

Disclaimer: This guide is informational only and not legal, tax, immigration, medical, financial or professional advice. Rules can change. Always check the official Austrian source before applying.

Many newcomers need an account for salary, rent, utilities, insurance and everyday payments. Some use an existing SEPA account at first, while others need an Austrian account quickly.

This guide does not rank banks or recommend products. Bank rules and fees change, so check provider terms before applying.

1. Why you may need an account

An account can support salary payments, rent payments, direct debits, card payments and official communication with providers.

2. Documents banks may ask for

Banks may ask for identification, address information, tax details, proof of employment or study and residence-related information. Requirements vary by provider.

3. Online banks versus traditional banks

Online banks may be convenient and fast. Traditional banks may offer branches and local support. Compare support, fees, verification and whether the account works for your salary and rent.

4. Salary payments

Ask your employer what banking details they need. If you use a non-Austrian account, confirm it is acceptable for payroll.

5. Rent payments

Landlords may expect transfers from a reliable account. Keep payment confirmations and make sure your rent reference is clear.

6. Caution about fees

Check monthly fees, card fees, ATM fees, transfer fees and currency fees. Low headline prices do not always mean low total cost.

Practical checklist

  • Decide what the account must do.
  • Prepare ID and address documents.
  • Compare online and traditional options.
  • Check salary and rent compatibility.
  • Read fees carefully.
  • Use official bank channels only.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Choosing only by online popularity.
  • Ignoring fees.
  • Assuming every account works for payroll.
  • Applying before documents are ready.
  • Clicking suspicious banking links.

FAQ

Do I need an Austrian address?

Some providers may ask for address information or registration evidence. Check the bank's current requirements.

Are online banks accepted?

Often, but verify salary, rent and official-use needs before relying on one.

Does this site rank banks?

No. It explains how to compare options without unsupported rankings.
